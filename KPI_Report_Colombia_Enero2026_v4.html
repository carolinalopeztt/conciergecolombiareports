About this KPI · January 2026
KPI 4 was restructured to focus on in-operation service errors (where client impact is highest), replacing the previous booking accuracy version. The Error Log documents designed by Giulia are the current source of truth for both cities. January serves as the reference point — formal error rate measurement begins February 2026.

📁 Error Log Folder: View Error Log Documents — CTG & MDE
Error Categories
Two Travel Error
Our communication, coordination, confirmation, or oversight directly caused the issue. Requires a documented process change within 72 hours.
Shared / Preventable
Supplier or client caused the immediate error, but a Two Travel process gap allowed it to happen. Requires identifying the specific gap.
Outside Our Control
Genuine supplier failure or unforeseen circumstance no reasonable TT process could prevent. Valid — but not a permanent exemption for recurring vendors.

Cross-KPI Insights
Connections Between Performance Areas
KPI 2 + KPI 5 share root causes
Late itinerary deliveries (KPI 2, 64%) and late pre-bills (KPI 5, 86%) stem from the same gap: itineraries getting finalized last-minute even when planning started well in advance. Pascalle is the clearest case — kick-off Dec 2, arrival Jan 6, itinerary Dec 14 (KPI 2 ✗), pre-bill Jan 4 (KPI 5 ✗). Multi-city trips and far-advance bookings need earlier internal milestones, not just a 7-day deadline at the end.
KPI 3 reflects exceptional in-destination execution
Despite process gaps in KPI 2 and KPI 5, all 16 reviews are 5 stars. The team consistently delivers outstanding in-destination experiences even when pre-trip planning fell behind. As volume scales, this reliance on individual excellence is the thing that KPI 4 error tracking is designed to protect — catching systematic issues before they reach the guest.
KPI 1 baseline: what to focus on to grow +20%
Baseline: $2,878/client · $344/pax · 14.3 services/client. The highest-leverage moves for +20%: (1) more proactive service recommendations at intake, especially for large groups like Rick Kwan; (2) offering CTG+MDE itineraries to qualifying single-city bookings — multi-city clients averaged $3,919/client vs $2,457 for single-city; (3) earlier preference capture to close untapped potential (Linda: 5 services, Inna: 8 services on 10-day trips).
