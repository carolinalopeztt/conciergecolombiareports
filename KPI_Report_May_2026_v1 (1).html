KPI 1 · Revenue & Services
Revenue per Client & Services Confirmed
Revenue reflects what clients paid from which Two Travel earns profit: concierge fees, transport, tours, activities, chef/cook, wellness, entertainment, and special services. Excluded: concierge fees, villas/houses, and boats (sold by the sales team, not the concierge team); Jr. concierge accompaniment (no TT gain); restaurant/venue deposits (client passthroughs — to be eliminated with the new direct payment link process); grocery budget (passthrough); and tips.

Commission figures are approximate — amounts are taken from venue consumption invoices converted at the same COP/USD rate used in each client's pre-bill or final invoice. Commission invoices must be requested the same day as the event and followed up consistently — the longer we wait, the harder it becomes for providers to locate and send the information. This also helps identify venues that systematically delay invoices so we can address it with them directly. Several commission invoices for May are still outstanding.

Claude Scores · A Learning Tool for Non-Responders
ℹ
The 2 clients who did not respond to the WhatsApp rating (Tatiana Berman and Ayo Cole) are not counted in the KPI. Their Claude scores are published here as a team learning tool. → Non-response rate is a signal, not a data gap
Non-response means different things depending on context. Ayo's non-response correlates with documented friction in KPI 4 — a pattern worth watching. Tatiana's is different: a 4-time client with a longstanding relationship who simply engages informally. Over time, tracking whether non-response tends to cluster around clients with operational issues (vs. loyal returning clients) will make the response rate a more meaningful signal.
✓ Claude scores = team growth, not KPI weight
The Claude scores are grounded in check-out reports and KPI 4 data, and are always clearly labeled so they're never confused with client-provided ratings. Their value is qualitative: they help the team reflect on every trip, not just the ones that generated a response. As the check-out report process matures, these scores will become a richer source of insight for how to continuously improve.
