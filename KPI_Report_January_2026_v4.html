KPI 4 · Service Reliability & Process Improvement
Execution Quality & Error Reduction
Track all service errors that occur during client trips, categorize them by accountability tier, and use that data to systematically reduce preventable errors over time. This KPI focuses on in-operation execution quality — where errors carry the highest client impact — and on building the process discipline that prevents recurrence.
Tracking in progress
About this KPI · January 2026
KPI 4 was fully restructured — the previous version tracked booking accuracy; this version focuses on in-operation service errors, where client impact is highest. The Error Log documents (designed by Giulia) are the current source of truth for both cities, and I'm in the process of automating and streamlining how we collect and visualize this data over time.

📁 Error Log Folder (both cities): View Error Log Documents — CTG & MDE
Error Categories
Two Travel Error
A mistake in our communication, coordination, confirmation, or oversight directly caused the issue. This category requires a documented process change within 72 hours.
Shared / Preventable
A supplier or client caused the immediate error, but a Two Travel process gap allowed it to happen or go uncaught. Requires identifying the specific process gap.
Outside Our Control
A genuine supplier quality failure, unforeseen circumstance, or client decision that no reasonable Two Travel process could have prevented. Valid — but not a permanent exemption for recurring vendors.
⚠
Categorization integrity: The person responsible for an error should not make the final categorization call alone. A second team member or city lead validates the classification. Disputes escalate to Caro + Giulia depending on the city.

A documented Two Travel Error with a completed process change is not a failure on the KPI — it's evidence the system is working. A month with zero errors and no process changes logged may indicate underreporting, not operational excellence.

Cross-KPI Insights
Connections Between Performance Areas
KPI 2 + KPI 5 share root causes
Late itinerary deliveries (KPI 2, 64%) and late pre-bills (KPI 5, 86%) stem from the same core issue: itineraries being finalized late when planning started well in advance. Pascalle is the clearest example — kick-off Dec 2, arrival Jan 6 (35 days lead), yet itinerary sent Dec 14 (KPI 2 ✗) and pre-bill Jan 4 (KPI 5 ✗). KPI 3 reflects exceptional execution
Despite planning timing gaps in KPI 2 and KPI 5, all 16 reviews are 5 stars. The team consistently compensates for pre-trip complexity with outstanding in-destination delivery. As volume scales, this reliance on individual effort becomes a risk — which is why the KPI 4 error tracking structure matters going forward.
KPI 5 at 86% — closer than it looks
Once same-week clients (Dave Young) are correctly counted as ✓ — invoice paid before check-in — the real gap is 2 clients out of 14. Both Pascalle (35 days lead time) and Allison (56 days lead time) had enough runway; the issue is that multi-city complexity and high volume pushed finalization to the last days. 95% target = 13/14 clients; one more case resolved and we're there.
